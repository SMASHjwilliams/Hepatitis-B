The Science Behind Hepatitis

Hepatitis B is an enveloped DNA virus. This virus replicates in the liver cell membrane. Hepatitis B can be transmitted by direct contact like sexual contact. There is a difference between acute hepatitis B and chronic hepatitis B. Acute hepatitis B is when a person is first infected with the hepatitis B virus. More than 90% of infants that are infected will develop a chronic Hepatitis B infection. Some symptoms of Hepatitis B is that peoples eyes turns yellow, experience a lot of pain, and the urine has a dark color.
